The Landscapes of New South Wales

Blue Mountains National Park

Image Source

The Blue Mountains National Park is a protected national park that is located in the Blue Mountains region of New South Wales, in eastern Australia.The national park is one of the eight protected areas that in 2000 it was decalred as part of UNESCO world heritage sites.The national park was a proposal by early conservationist Myles Dunphy for a Greater Blue Mountains National Park in 1932.Activities for the visitora include short walks to lookouts above cliffs and more extreme sports such as canyoning,abseiling,rock climbing and mountain biking.

Budawangs National Park

Image Source

The Blue Mountains National Park is a protected national park that is located in the Blue Mountains region of New South Wales, in eastern Australia.The park is named after Mount Budawang.The mountain istelf derives the name from Aboriginal word 'Buddawong',the vantage point by the mountain was originally used to make signal fires.Budawang National Park's diverse landscape has created pockets of unique habitats where several plant and animal species survive.The park has a good population of swamp wallabies,greater gliders and potoroos.

Opera House

Image Source

The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts centre at Sydney Harbour located in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia.Designed by a Danish architect but completed by an Australian architectural team,it was formally opened in 1973.The building can hold multiple events in different venues,can host well over 1500 events and 1.2 million people anually.In June 2007 the Opera House became a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

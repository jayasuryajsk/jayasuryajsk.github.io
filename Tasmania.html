The Landscapes of Tasmania

Freycinet National Park

Image Source

World Heritage listed Freycinet National Park is one of Australia's oldest nature reserves.It is on the east coast of Tasmania.The tourist attraction of this peninsula is a perfect curve of powder-white sand beach and the sea.Throughout the park, hiking trails lead through bushland and bays.The Coles Bay is a good base for walks and climbs in the surrounding hills.

Tasman National Park

Image Source

This park is located 56kms east of Hobart and has some of Australia's most spectacular coastal scenery.There is nothing but ocean between Tasman National Park and Antarctica.The Blowhole and Tasman Arch are two of the park's most famous features.It has diverse wildlife including seals,dolphins,whales,fairy penguins and rare birds.

Bruny Island

Image Source

Bruny island is a popular day trip for the foodies.It is famous for treats such as handmande choclates,local berries,seafood and cheese.The island offers beautiful coastal scenery with soaring green cliffs and beaches.The island is home to many land animals such as fairy penguins,wombats,wallabies and echidnas.Built by convicts between 1836 and 1838,Cape Bruny Lighthouse offers beautiful views of the surging Southern Ocean.

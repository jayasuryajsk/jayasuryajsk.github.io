The Landscapes of Queensland

Windin Falls

Image Source

Windin Falls is located in a remote area of Wooroonooran National Park.It is 1367km northwest of Brisbane and is one of the wet tropics world heritage area series of national parks.The national park contains some of the oldest surviving rainforests in the world.The hike of the Windin Falls can take up to approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes each way of the falls with a muddy trail.

Wallaman Falls

Image Source

The Wallaman Falls is located in the northern region of Queensland and is one of the UNESCO world heritage sites.The waterfall is famous for it's 268 meters main drop.The geological history of the formation can be traced back to 50 million years.It is the country's tallest single-drop waterfall.

Great Barrier Reef

Image Source

The Great Barrier Reef is world's largest coral reef with more than 900 islands.It is located on Coral sea, off the coast of Queensland.The reef supports variety of wildlife, including many vulnerable and endangered species.It is very popular destination for tourists and is an important economic activity for the region,generating over 3 billion AUD per year.

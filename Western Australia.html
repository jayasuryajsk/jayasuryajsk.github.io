The Landscapes of Western Australia

Broome

Image Source

The tourist town of Broome is one of the most popular Western Australia's destinations.The main attraction Cable Beach is backed by red cliffs,sweeping white sands and turqouise waters.Broome is also Australia's pearling capital.The Broome Historical Museum is collection of fascinating history of the town.

Ningaloo Reef Marine Park

Image source

UNESCO World Heritage site, Ningaloo Reef is the world's largest fringing reef and spans for about 260kms.One of the top beaches for snorkeling is Turquoise Bay,a strech of white sand and crystal-clear water.It is one of the few places in the world where you can swim with whale sharks.This place can be easily accessed by shore unlike the Great Barrier Reef.

Wave Rock

Image Source

The Wave Rock is a rock formation of bamded granite, 15 meters high, in the form of a sea tide.The rock contains different shades of gray and red colours.It is also known as Katter Kich by Noongar Aboriginal Australians.Wave Rock is a part of Hyden Wildlife Park which attracts more than 100,000 visitors every year.
